Privacy Policy for Mockey Patcher Chrome Extension

Last Updated: August 12, 2024

Introduction

Mockey Patcher ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Chrome extension.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide

The Mockey Patcher extension does not collect any personal information that you directly provide to us.

Automatically Collected Information

When you use our Chrome extension, we may collect the following information:

  • Network Requests: The extension captures HTTP/HTTPS requests made by websites you visit for the purpose of mocking API responses. This includes URLs, request headers, and response data.
  • Mock Configurations: Any mock configurations you create within the extension are stored locally in your browser's storage and optionally in the backend database if you choose to use it.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain the functionality of the Mockey Patcher extension
  • To enable the creation and management of mock API responses
  • To improve and optimize the extension's performance and user experience

Data Storage

The Mockey Patcher extension stores data in the following ways:

  • Local Storage: Most data is stored locally in your browser using Chrome's Storage API. This data does not leave your device unless you explicitly choose to export it.
  • Backend Database: If you choose to use the backend service, mock configurations may be stored in a database that you control. The backend service is self-hosted, meaning you have full control over where and how this data is stored.

Data Sharing

We do not share your data with any third parties. All data captured by the extension remains on your device or within your self-hosted backend service.

Security

We take reasonable measures to help protect your information from loss, theft, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. However, no method of electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your Rights

Since all data is stored locally on your device or in your self-hosted backend, you have complete control over your data. You can:

  • View all data stored by the extension through the Chrome DevTools
  • Delete any or all data through the extension's interface
  • Clear all extension data through Chrome's extension management interface

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.
